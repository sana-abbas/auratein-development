We’ve come across Shamim Rajani from Pakistan, and we were fascinated by how she not only runs a successful agency, but also organizes a coding bootcamp for young women. From the very beginning, we’ve seen great potential in a collaboration, both by helping to finance more women to participate in the bootcamp, as well as working with graduates of the same. In spring 2019, we started collaborating with Shamim, and one year later, Auratein development was born. The foundation was a bit of money, visionary ideas and a ton of motivation.

“CodeGirls” is a coding bootcamp for young Pakistani women from all kinds of backgrounds. It teaches them the foundation of programming with a different focus depending on the strengths of the participants. These can be database management, user experience or others. Apart from the technical skills, the participants also acquire organizational skills, such as project management.

“Auratein” means “women” in urdu which underlines our philosophy “from women for women”. Our vision is to create jobs in tech for women in a country where gender equality is not yet present, and to give them the opportunity to be successful in this industry. We would like to be an example to show that teams can function across countries, religions and ethnicities.

We compensate our developers with an above average salary in their market and put a lot of focus on their education and career development, as well as personal growth.

At the same time, some of our developers are at their early career state. While we want them to learn as much as possible by implementing customer projects, we ensure quality control by close collaboration with experienced developers.

All members of our team have different backgrounds with one thing in common: we are eager to learn and evolve. By having people with different perspectives, it’s easy for us to adapt to different customer requirements.

We provide a fair and flexible work place for all our employees which is important because some of us are mothers and need to balance family and job. It is also important to us that our employees advance in their career development which is why we support advanced training and participating in local events.

We hope to help build a community of brilliant women in tech in Pakistan, and to provide them with a safe work environment. We want to be a role model for good workplace conditions and hope to inspire other communities to follow suit.
